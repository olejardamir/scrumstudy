SCRUM

Definition: Framework for production management and development. Founded on Empiricism (experience) with an iterative/incremental approach (definition of "Done").

1.0 PILLARS

1.1 Transparency

Definition: Use of a common language and definitions (for example, definition of "Done").

1.2 Inspection

Definition: Inspection of progress and artifacts.

1.3 Adaption

Definition: Adjustments to minimize deviation.

 

2.0 TEAM

2.1 Product Owner

What does the Product Owner do?

-Manages backlog, optimizes value of Dev. Team, shows what scrum team to work on next, Makes sure that dev. Team understands the items, 1 person, may represent desires of a committee, Addressed regarding backlog changes, tells dev. Team to work on different requirements (and nobody else), cancels sprints (an nobody else).

2.2 Development Team

Definition: Self-managing, cross-functional, developers (no sub-teams) 3-9 professionals (no master/owner) accountable for a development.

- Delivers the releasable increment of "Done" (End of Sprint), while nobody else tells how to turn backlog into "Done".

2.3 Scrum Master

Definition: Servant/Leader to a team, may work with other scrum masters to maximize effectiveness.

What does the Scrum Master do?

-Ensures that team follows Scrum rules, maximizes team's value, understands product planning, agility/facilitates scrum events.

Relationship to a Product Owner:

-Finds techniques for backlog management keeping it clear and concise, and arranged to maximize value.

-Understanding product planning, practices agility and Facilitates Scrum events as requested or needed.

 

Relationship to a Development Team:

-helps team understand the clarity of a backlog, helps team deliver high-value product, helps teams adopt scrum.

-Coaches self-organization and cross-functionality, removes impediments in progress, facilitates scrum events.

 

Relationship to Organization:

-Coaches scrum adoption, plans scrum implementations, helps employees to understand scrum.

-Planning Scrum implementations , causes changes that increases the productivity and increases the effectiveness (with or without other Masters).

 

Relationship to Stake-Holders:

-Helps stakeholders understand scrum.

 

3.0 EVENTS (Sprint)

Definition: Minimized and time-boxed meetings designed for transparency and inspection (and adoption). Starts immediately after a conclusion of previous Sprint. Changes that endanger the sprint goal are not allowed, scope clarified and re-negotiated, backlog reviewed.

Duration: 1 month or less (with a releasable "Done" increment). Can be canceled before expiry (obsolete goals).

Scope may be clarified and re-negotiated between the Product Owner and Development Team .

Only the Product Owner has the authority to cancel the Sprint (maybe under influence from the stakeholders, the Development Team, or the Scrum Master).

 

3.1 Sprint Planning

Definition: Answers what can be delivered at increment, and how to achieve it. After forecasting backlog, goal is made.

Duration: 8 hrs. or less, for a 1-month sprint (less for shorter sprints).

What does the Product Owner do?

The Product Owner discusses the objective of Sprint and Product Backlog items to achieve the Sprint Goal.

 

What does the Scrum Master do?

- Ensures that the event takes place and that attendants understand its purpose, keeping it within time-box.

 

What does the Development Team do?

-Forecasts the functionality that will be developed during the Sprint by assessing what it can accomplish over the upcoming Sprint.

-Decides how it will build this functionality into a "Done" product Increment during the Sprint.

-Decomposes the backlog units to 1 day or less, by self-organizing.

-Determines it has too much or too little work, and if so, renegotiates with owner.

-May explain the steps that it will take to make the next increment ("Done").

 

3.2 Daily Scrum

Definition: Improves communications, eliminates other meetings, identifies impediments to development for removal, highlights and promote quick decision-making, and improves the Development Team's level of knowledge.

Duration: 15 minutes.

What does the Scrum Master do?

-Ensures that the Development Team has the meeting, keeping within a time-box.

What does the Development Team do?

-Responsible for conducting the Daily Scrum, enforces the rule that only Development Team participates.

-Synchronizes activities and creates a plan for the next 24 hours, inspecting a progress toward the Sprint Goal.

 

3.4 Sprint Review

Definition: Scrum team and stakeholders (optional) collaborate on what was done and what to do. Is informal meeting to gain a better insight and collaboration.

Duration: 4hrs. or less for 1-month sprint (or shorter for shorter sprints).

What does the Product Owner do?

-Can invite key Stake-Holders (informal), Explains what was and was not done, Discusses the current state of Product Backlog.

What does the Scrum Master do?

-May invite Stakeholders.

 

What does the Development Team do?

-Discusses what went well during the Sprint, what problems it ran into, and how those problems were solved; demonstrates the work that it has "Done" and answers questions about the Increment;

 

 

What is the role of Stake-Holders?

-Can attend, if and only if, invited by Scrum Master.

 

3.5 Sprint Retrospective

Definition: Team inspects itself and plans the next sprint.

Duration: 3hrs or less for a 1-month sprint (or shorter for shorter sprints).

What does the Scrum Master do?

-Participates as a peer team member in the meeting from the accountability over the Scrum process.

 

3.6 Goal

Definition: Provides guidance, created during sprint-planning. Backlog is re-negotiated with owner if goal doesn't work as predicted.

What does the Product Owner do?

-Decides whether to release after an increment.

What does the Development Team do?

-Implements the functionality and technology or renegotiates the sprint scope with owner.

 

4.0 ARTIFACTS

Definition: work/value for inspection/adaption (transparency).

 

What does the Product Owner do?

-Works with everyone to make sure that artifacts are transparent.

-Chooses whether to release after an increment.

 

What does the Scrum Master do?

- Works with the Product Owner, Development Team, and other involved parties to understand if the artifacts are transparent (learning, convincing, and change).

 

 

4.1 Product Backlog

Definition:  ordered list of everything that is known to be needed in the product.

Duration: Never complete.

What does the Product Owner do?

- Owner is responsible for the Product Backlog (content, availability, and ordering), and can update it at any time.

-Refines the backlog with Dev. Team.

-Monitors progress but does not make the final (work) estimates. Assesses the progress, making it transparent to all stakeholders.

 

What does the Scrum Master do?

What does the Development Team do?

- Refines the backlog with Product Owner, does all work estimates and forecasts.

-Modifies the Sprint Backlog throughout the Sprint, and the Sprint Backlog emerges during the Sprint.

 

4.2 Sprint Backlog

Definition: Set of product backlogs designed to convert backlog to "Done", while all units are decomposed to 1-day tasks (or less). Too much or too little work is re-negotiated with the product owner. Others may attend for a better insight. After it is established, team must be able to tell owner how to accomplish the sprint-goal.

 

What does the Product Owner do?

-The Product Owner is the sole person responsible (accountable) for managing the Product Backlog.

-May represent the desires of a committee in the Product Backlog.

-Is addressed if any change to be done to a Backlog.

-Express Backlog items, Orders items to achieve goal, optimize value of development team work, ensure transparency and understanding of items.

-No-one else can tell Development Team to work on different requirements.

 

What does the Development Team do?

-Express Backlog items, Orders items to achieve goal, optimize value of development team work, Ensure transparency and understanding of items.

-Does not act on anyone's requirements other than the ones of the product Owner.

-Only the Development Team can change its Sprint Backlog during a Sprint.

 

 

4.3 Increment

Definition: Next logical step completing a definition of "Done".
